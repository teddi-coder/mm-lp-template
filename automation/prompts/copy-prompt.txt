## OUTPUT REQUIRED

Write all of the following. Label each section clearly.

**1. HERO HEADLINE (H1)**
One punchy all-caps line. Must include the primary service and target suburb. 8 words max. This will render at large display size — it needs to hit hard.
Write 2 variants.

**2. HERO SUBHEADLINE**
One sentence. Expands on the headline — adds the trust angle or the key differentiator. 15–20 words. Sentence case.
Write 2 variants.

**3. HERO BODY COPY**
2–3 sentences. Speaks directly to what the customer is looking for, references the suburb/area, and introduces the workshop as the obvious choice. Include the phone number naturally if CTA is call-focused.

**4. HERO CTA TEXT**
If call: short urgent line above the phone number (e.g. "Call us today — same-day bookings available")
If form: form headline (e.g. "Get a free quote — we'll call you back within the hour")
If both: write one of each.

**5. TRUST BAR ITEMS**
4 short trust items for the horizontal trust bar. Format: [number or icon label] + [short descriptor].
Examples: "847 Five-Star Reviews", "20+ Years Experience", "RAA Approved Repairer"
Use the client's actual numbers. Make them specific.

**6. SERVICES SECTION HEADLINE**
One line. All caps. References the primary service + location.
Example: "LOGBOOK SERVICING IN NORWOOD & SURROUNDS"

**7. SERVICE DESCRIPTIONS**
For each service listed in the brief, write:
- Service name (bold label)
- 2–3 sentence description that explains what it covers and why this workshop is the right choice for it
- Keep it practical — what does the customer actually get?

**7b. SERVICE CHECKLIST HEADLINE**
One line. All caps. Names the service and implies completeness.
Example: "WHAT'S INCLUDED IN EVERY LOGBOOK SERVICE"

**7c. SERVICE CHECKLIST ITEMS**
8–12 specific items included in the primary service. Each item is a short noun phrase (3–6 words). These will render as a 2-column checklist grid with tick marks.
Examples: "Factory-specified oil and filter", "Tyre pressure and tread depth check", "Logbook stamped (warranty maintained)"
Use the service name to make them specific — not generic across all services.

**7d. BENEFIT CARDS HEADLINE**
One line. All caps. References the workshop's key advantage or the suburb.
Example: "WHY NORWOOD DRIVERS CHOOSE US"

**7e. BENEFIT CARDS (4 items)**
Four short benefit cards. Each has:
- Bold label (3–5 words — specific claim, not generic)
- 1–2 sentence description (what specifically, not vague promises)
These are different from selling points — they should address objections (warranty, surprise costs, availability, etc.)

**7f. HOW IT WORKS HEADLINE**
One short all-caps line.
Examples: "BOOKING IS EASY" / "HOW IT WORKS" / "READY IN THREE STEPS"

**7g. HOW IT WORKS STEPS (exactly 3)**

- Step label (short, 2–4 words, all caps)

Step 1: "CALL OR BOOK ONLINE" / "Call us on [PHONE] or fill in the form below. Tell us your car, the service you need, and a time that suits — we'll confirm the booking within the hour."
